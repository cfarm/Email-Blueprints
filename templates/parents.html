News you can use!

If your daughter is a senior...

Convocation and Commencement for the Class of 2012 will be held on Friday 12 May and Saturday 13 May.

The full schedule of activities for the most important weekend of your Mawrter's life may be found on our website. Lodging information, accessibility information, maps, directions and move out information may be found on the website. If you have questions, please do not hesitate to call the Information Desk at (610) 526-7330

Career Development Office
The Bi-Co Career Development Office offers students resume` review, networking services, career counseling help and a host of other services. If your daughter has not yet connected with the "CDO" it is not too late to get started. Bryn Mawr offers current students and alumnae help in every aspect of the career search. Visit their website to learn about all they have to offer!

If your daughter is a junior...

Fellowships applications and Summer Funding applications, particularly the Hanna Holborn Gray Summer Internship Grant applications, are coming due.

Get to know the CDO! If your daughter hasn't been talking about the externship program, or the recruiters who come to campus, or the networking events, now is a good time to tell her to make some friends in the Career Development Office. The CDO is a Bi-Co office and offers many services for career preparation as well as preparation for graduate school.

And, Room Draw!

If your daughter is a sophomore...

Study abroad applications are due this spring. There are various deadlines contingent upon program. Visit the Study Abroad website to learn more.

Sophomores need to declare a major this spring, or submit an application to complete an independent major. Click on the links to learn more about advising and the major process.

If your student has a passion for research, or wants to explore something new, summer funding is available. Information sessions for several funding opportunities are offered in January, deadlines for Summer Funding applications are due in February and March.

Fellowships info sessions and deadlines are coming up fast. Sophomores need to learn about these important opportunities now, so that they are poised to compete next fall and spring.

If your daughter is a first year...

Remember those nice people who welcomed your daughter to campus? They were customspeople. This spring is the time for your daughter to consider becoming a customsperson. Applications will be due this spring as well as applications to become Community Diversity Assistants, Tour Guides, Student Bloggers and a host of other positions on campus.

"It's a Bryn Mawr thing."

Let's Talk About Room Draw ( I think we should ask a student Traditions Mistress, Customs to be a guest writer every month. Topic of her choice...Hell Week? May Day? Club Canaday? Special Dinners?)

What can one say about Room Draw? It is fair. It is complicated. It develops strategic thinking skills, (ie. if you are willing to share you might qualify for some sweet digs). It can be stressful, fun, and did we say it can be complicated? Asking your daughter about room draw during the room draw frenzy is a roll of the dice. Liken it to the following questions from your past experience.
  • How is your college essay coming along?
  • Do you think you'll be going to prom?
  • Would you like me to make the appointment for your second driver's license test, or do you want to make it yourself?
However the conversation goes, rest assured, we have a place for her!
